Join him on the adventure of a lifetime in THE GRADES.

JailBreak

Home. That was the only thing on multi-billionaire toy company CEO, Oliver Ullyses’s (Stephen Amell) mind after being falsely accused and arrested for the murder of his long-time rival. After three long years in jail he breaks out and embarks on a perilous journey to return to the life he once had. But he can’t do it alone. With the help of family friend Eugene Maes (Dane DeHaan) and his former advisor Sully Choure (Tim DeKay) with aid of a mysterious new ally he makes the trip home. However, someone is after him, someone powerful, someone who wants him dead, someone who will stop at nothing.

The One The Only

After refusing to break up with his poor girlfriend Selena (Selena Cheung), valiant Justin Lartes (Foram Patel) is forced to enter the 56th Selection Games- a suicide competition where 8 brave men compete to win Princess Clara’s (Keith Yeung) hand in marriage. Despite the appealing advantages that come with marrying the princess, Justin is not competing for her, but for his freedom. He must go through a series of grueling obstacles including fighting to the death to prove his etiquette, intelligence, leadership and strength. Losing the games is not an option for Justin as it results in execution. All he wishes for is the chance to run away with Selena. Will Justin survive the gruesome games and return to his true love?

Trapped Inside

Odyssea, played by Xandria Crosland, races against time to get to her basketball game. As she leaves Townsend Harris High School, she has to go through numerous nerve-racking obstacles posed by her teachers, Mr. Dundun and Mr. Bardun. Escaping from her nightmare every flight of stairs she goes, trouble awaits for her. There is a never ending cycle of fear and unpredictable events ahead of her. Each step that Odyssea takes towards the doors of freedom, the feeling of trepidation rushes into her. Why are her teachers after this girl? Will Odyssea ever get to her game on time and escape from hell? Odyssea thought school was tough. Now she has learned that leaving it is tougher.

Number 10

HE’S NOT THE FIRST, BUT HE MAY BE THE LAST. It’s the year 2075, where 0.17% of modernized America’s population is clinically disabled. Enter a dystopian world where being handicapped is frowned upon and experimentation for the “greater good” is standard. Zoom in a little further into the life of a crippled, and loved, husband and father. #10 wakes up to find himself captured and in the process of being tested on. Follow his journey as he tries to get back home. Throw in an evil scientist, a secretly kind accomplice, and a son who is ready to dig up some dirt, and things get a lot more complicated. He's not the first, but he may be the last.

Freedom

In 1847, in the Deep South, Olivia is a domestic slave on the Eden Plantation. She is abused by her owner and lives a life of turmoil. However, she uses her cleverness, tenacity, and her friendship with the master’s daughter to aid her on her pursuit of freedom. She embarks on a dangerous journey to save her family from being sold to another plantation, so that she may fulfill her dream to reunite with them after many years of separation. Will she be able to write her own story or will the chains of slavery weigh her down?

Going Home

Home (noun) - a place where one lives permanently, especially as a member of a family or household. Home is a place that brings comfort and peace to one's mind. Except for 14 year-old Mason. Home to him, for as long as he could remember, is a place of dread and hopelessness. For over ten years, Mason is confined to his “father's” basement where his only hope is to go outside. Yet, when Mason discovers the truth behind his past, he escapes and goes on an epic journey filled with friendship, fear, and pain. All to return home to be with his real family... that is if he makes it!

The Escape

Life flashes before your eyes when you’re about to die. But for Captain Oh Ji Yeong of the South Korean army, life flashes every second of the day. He never planned on getting stuck in a North Korean prison camp during the North-South Korean War of 2040, but a failed rescue mission begins his grueling confinement in the unforgiving labor camps. As bullets pierce the bodies of soldiers crawling across the battlefield, Oh Ji Yeong grows weary of the torture, desperately longing to return to his beloved wife and child back at home. However, North Korea isn’t going to make liberation easy for him; little does the war captain know about the multitude of obstacles that lurk in every shadow of his path. The question remains: will he ever be able to see the light of a new day?

Tangled in Time

It’s 1981 and Alec is way ahead of his time. In fact, he has his whole life set out right in front of him: he’s attending the country’s most prestigious university, he’s found a girl that he truly thinks is the one, and he has a supportive family that believes in his crazy lifelong dream of achieving time travel. However, as if things couldn’t have gotten any better, his dream isn’t so crazy anymore when it becomes his reality. Alec has perfected time travel.

Unfortunately, all good things must come to an end. When Alec’s jealous “best friend”, Jafar, intentionally tampers with Alec’s time machine, Alec’s life goes awry. Sucked into present-day New York, with his family worried sick about Alec’s well-being and Michelle, his love interest, constantly being hounded by other men, Alec will have to fight many action-packed battles. How will he untangle the knots of time?
